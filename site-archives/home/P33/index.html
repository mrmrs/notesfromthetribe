Notes from 03.20.12

Short of forcing every performer to eat Bagels and smoked whitefish on camera, I think one would be hard pressed to find anything that could make the the Chabad ‘To Life’ telethon more ‘Jewish’.

The program is well known for its Bearded Hassidic Rabbis in black hats ebulliently dancing to Klezmer in front of a giant tote board and portrait of the Rebbe whilst a cavalcade of celebrities try their best Catskills comedic routine or sing traditional yiddish folk songs.

And all in the name of charity.  Over the course of the 31 broadcasts, they’ve raised millions of dollars for social services that range from education to rehabilitation.

Using their influence to help countless people from all backgrounds.
Like I said, typically Jewish.

For more information visit : http://tolife.com/
